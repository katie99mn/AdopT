Ruby Slavin

Hi, my name is Ruby Slavin and I am a third-year undergraduate student studying Neurobiology and Psychology. When I’m not studying, I am an avid reader and movie-watcher, but I also love to get outdoors and spend time with nature, or friends, or both!

Establishing AdopT. Club at UW–Madison, along with the entire leadership team, has been a truly special and fulfilling enterprise. I was born in Fuzhou, a city in southeastern Jiangxi province, China. I was adopted at 18 months and brought to the United States, where I grew up in a suburb of Minneapolis, Minnesota. I grew up in a bicultural household, my mom being born and raised in Argentina and my dad Fargo, North Dakota. Before they adopted me, my mom had my two older brothers. Since attending university, I’ve explored and learned more about my Asian American identity, something I was unfamiliar with growing up. I am excited to build a community of adoptees at UW–Madison and throughout the greater Madison area, while advocating for and empowering individuals with the unique adoptee identity.
