Find & Build Community.

Our Mission

"The mission of the Transracial Adoptees at UW - Madison is to create a safe and welcoming environment for adoptees and those impacted by the adoption system to share their experiences and join in community, as well as those with an intentional interest to engage in the adoptee identity. We offer space for individuals to engage in social, educational, and service activities pertaining to adoption while promoting adoptees participation in multicultural spaces on campus. Our vision is to destigmatize and demystify discussions around adoption while holding space for adoptees to find pride in their unique and collective narratives. We welcome all who are interested in engaging in sharing ideas, thoughts, and stories around adoption and are guided by inclusivity and belonging for all."

- AdopT. Club

David Izzo

Hi! My name is David Izzo, and I am a sophomore studying Political Science and Environmental Studies. Outside of AdopT, you can find me with the Mock Trial team, cooking with my roommates, watching TV, or spending time with friends!

I was adopted from Seoul, South Korea. I was around 6-10 months old when I made the flight back with my family, finding my home in the Chicago suburbs. I have a younger sister who was also adopted from South Korea, as well as a connection to other adoptees in the area. This connection was from a summer camp for South Korean adoptees, a community that gave me a bit of shelter from the questions, misunderstandings, and assumptions I was familiar with. When I came to college, I wanted to give that opportunity to the community to adoptees who may have never had it. Especially in an environment that is new and far away to many, it is important to find community. For adoptees, we are not always provided the avenues to find that home away from home. So, we must make it. I am proud to stand with this incredible board and wonderful community as we work to pave a path forward together.

Grace Drayton

Hello!

I’m Grace Drayton, currently a senior at the University of Wisconsin – Madison pursuing a Bachelors of Business Administration in Management and Human Resources along with a certificate in Asian American Studies. A few of my passions and hobbies include exploring and experimenting to cook recipes from various global cuisines, running outdoors, and traveling.

Establishing AdopT. Club at UW–Madison, along with the leadership team, has been a truly special and fulfilling endeavor. I was born in Fuzhou, a city in southeastern Jiangxi province, China. I was adopted at 18 months and brought to the United States, where I grew up in a suburb of Minneapolis, Minnesota. I grew up in a bicultural household, my mom being born and raised in Argentina and my dad Fargo, North Dakota. Before they adopted me, my mom had my two older brothers. Since attending university, I’ve explored and learned more about my Asian American identity, something I was unfamiliar with growing up. I am excited to build a community of adoptees at UW–Madison and throughout the greater Madison area, while advocating for and empowering individuals with the unique adoptee identity.

Lacey Coyne

Hello! My name is Lacey Coyne and I am a fourth year pursuing a Bachelors in Consumer Behavior and Marketplace Studies with certificates in Entrepreneurship and Business Fundamentals. In my free time, I love cooking, listening to music, watching basketball, meeting new people, traveling, and cleaning to avoid homework!

Adopted from Anhui Province China, at 18 months old, by an entirely blond hair and light eyes family, I knew that I was different from those that surrounded me. As human beings, we crave belonging in the spaces we are in. As thankful as I am for my friends and family, belonging in my adoptive identity has been something I longed for. Not culturally immersed in my Chinese culture to feel “Asian enough” but obviously not white was a consistent thought. As I have aged, the more I gained interest in exploring and learning about this unique identity! When organizing Transracial Adoptees at UW-Madison, we were driven to create this space of belonging for individuals to feel empowered in their identities. The leadership team is beyond excited for this new journey!

Ruby Slavin

Hi, my name is Ruby Slavin and I am a third-year undergraduate student studying Neurobiology and Psychology. When I’m not studying, I am an avid reader and movie-watcher, but I also love to get outdoors and spend time with nature, or friends, or both!

I was adopted from Yueyang, Hunan, China when I was eleven months old and grew up in a suburb of Milwaukee. This suburb of Milwaukee was predominantly White, so I had few people I could share my identity as a transracial adoptee with. I quite often experienced the feeling of never being White enough but also never being Chinese enough to fit in. This cultural dysphoria followed me to university, but by working with AdopT. Club, I hope to create a space for fellow transracial adoptees to find a community with whom they can share their stories with. I strive to ensure that members feel heard, seen, and supported as they spend more time exploring their shared identity. I also hope to grant others who do not share the identity the opportunity to explore what it means to be a transracial adoptee, so we can create a more connected and empathetic community.

Shu Lan Schaut

Hi! My name is Shu Lan and I am a junior studying Neurobiology and English Literature. In my free time, I love to bake, read (fiction preferably!), and spend time with my friends. I also like to try and explore more of Madison whenever I can, from trying new restaurants to enjoying the outdoors.

I was adopted from Fencheng in the Jiangxi province of China when I was 14 months old and grew up in Kenosha, WI. Growing up in the Midwest as a person of color can be pretty isolating at times, even more so as a transracial adoptee in a White family. As a transracial adoptee, at times it feels like you are a part of separate two worlds, yet are not truly belonging in either. AdopT has created an opportunity for me to connect with other people who have felt the same and can relate to that unique perspective. In my role in AdopT, I hope I can help fellow transracial adoptees feel a greater sense of belonging at UW.
